# Contributing to Bitcoin History Reference

Thank you for your interest in contributing. This document explains the editorial standard, entry format, and how to submit changes.

## What Belongs Here

- People, organizations, companies, events, and cultural moments with a documented role in Bitcoin's history
- Entries should be verifiable — every claim should trace back to a primary source, published article, filing, or documented record
- Bitcoin-focused; altcoins only where directly relevant to Bitcoin's history (e.g., a figure who was influential in Bitcoin before or after an altcoin detour)

## What Does Not Belong Here

- Editorial opinion ("X is the most important contributor")
- Superlatives without documented basis ("the largest," "the first," unless sourced)
- Invented causal connections ("X inspired Y to build Z") unless documented
- Price predictions, investment advice, or promotional language
- Entries about altcoin projects with no meaningful Bitcoin connection

## Editorial Standard

Entries are **factual and descriptive only**. Write what someone did, not what it means or how important it was. A reader should be able to verify every sentence.

**Avoid:**
- "One of the most important figures in Bitcoin history"
- "Revolutionized the way people think about money"
- "Satoshi was inspired by X" (undocumented)

**Prefer:**
- "Co-authored the Lightning Network whitepaper (2016)"
- "Served as CEO from 2014 to 2022"
- "The fund raised $35 million in Series B funding in May 2023"

## Entry Format

Each entry follows this schema:

```
## Name — Organization or Alias (birth year–present or dates unknown)
**Tier:** 1, 2, or 3
**Role:** Short description of their role
**Active:** Year range (e.g., 2013–present)

One or more prose paragraphs. Factual and descriptive. No Hook field. No "Why It Matters" field.

**Sources:**
- Primary sources, books, articles, filings, documentation
```

**Tiers:**
- **Tier 1** — Foundational: cypherpunk precursors, Satoshi, earliest contributors
- **Tier 2** — Builders: developers, exchanges, wallets, Lightning, hardware, infrastructure, funding orgs
- **Tier 3** — Catalysts: investors, media, politicians, circular economies, antagonists, cultural moments

## How to Submit a Contribution

1. **Fork** this repository (click Fork in the top right on GitHub)
2. Make your edits in your fork
3. Submit a **Pull Request** with a brief description of what you added or changed and why
4. Include at least one source for any new factual claim

## Types of Contributions Welcome

- **New entries** for figures or events not yet covered
- **Corrections** to factual errors (with sources)
- **Additional sources** for existing entries
- **Expanded prose** for stub-like entries, staying within the editorial standard
